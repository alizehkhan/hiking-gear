BACKPACK

Osprey Sirrus 50 Womens

£150 | 1600g

Choice of footwear and backpack are the two things you do not skimp on, money wise or time spent finding the right one. These two pieces of gear will either carry you through your hike or drag you down.

A well-fitted pack can take kilos of pressure off your hips, shoulders, back and knees. For long-distance hikes I would say 40-60 litres is the range of capacity you'd be looking at (depending on how minimalist you are with packing). Try and go as small as you possibly can to encourage smart and light packing. A loaded pack shouldn't weigh more than about 20% of your body weight.

Osprey are like the Rolls Royce of traditional packs. Features I personally like are:
- suspension system which creates space between the pack and my sweaty back and also helps lighten the load;
- front shovel pocket to stash my rain jacket;
- lid and hip belt pockets for my snacks;
- gear loops for my trekking poles and hanging my washed socks to dry; and
- padded shoulder straps and hip belt.

With added features comes added weight so think about which ones you need. If you're not bothered about features (and have the money) definitely worth considering ultralight packs.

Choice of footwear and backpack are the two things you do not skimp on, money wise or time spent finding the right one. These two pieces of gear will either carry you through your hike or drag you down.

The most popular choice for hiking footwear are hiking shoes, trail runners or a hybrid of the two. Factors to consider when deciding which one, are weight, support, protection, durability, traction and breathability. Your specific requirements for each of these factors will depend on your trail's terrain, weather and your personal support preferences.

The hiking shoe option comes in 3 forms: the traditional high cut boot, the mid-cut boot and the low-cut shoe. The main benefit of hiking shoes are its durability, traction, protection and support. They have stiffer soles. The high-cut hiking boot provides unmatched ankle support. However, hiking shoes are heavier, remember one pound on your feet equals five pounds on your back.

Trekking poles help reduce the forces placed on your body which is important when you're carrying a heavy pack (and especially important if you have an injury!). But they'll only help you if you use them properly - learn how to grip them, the height and angle they should be at for uphill, downhill and on level ground.

I knew I wanted something that was lightweight for a decent price, had cork grips (which absorb sweat better / less slippery than synthetic grips) and were foldable to take on the plane without having to check them in. These poles ticked all the boxes for me and rated well.

WATER SYSTEM

On the trail

WATER SYSTEM

Katadyn BeFree Water Filter

£40

Katadyn BeFree is a drink-through filter attached to a soft bottle. I also keep a 1L Smart water bottle with a sports cap (they're light and durable).

Note: most filters only filter out protozoa and bacteria (microorganisms), not viruses. Viruses are too small and will pass through the pores of a filter. If you are hiking in an area where you're concerned about viruses you'll need a purifier to treat your filtered water. There are chemical purifiers, UV purifiers and filters that come with purifiers. If you do choose to use a chemical purifier, I'd recommend you bring something to flavour that water with if you're not a fan of the chemical aftertaste.

HIKING OUTFIT

Clothing

HIKING OUTFIT

Karrimor Aspen Tech T-Shirt Ladies Quechua Women's Mountain Hiking Shorts

£20

You only need one outfit for hiking, it'll always be sweaty and dirty. Stay away from cotton. Cotton can absorb around 20 times its own weight in water. Once wet, it becomes heavy, takes forever to dry and no longer insulates. You either want merino wool or a synthetic fibre such as nylon or polyester.

Merino wool is the better alternative, it's highly breathable, insulates even when wet, quick drying and naturally odour-resistant. This is because the merino sheep’s glands produce a natural wax called lanolin which lines the fibres and is naturally anti-bacterial. Synthetics are the cheaper alternative, they are quicker to dry but not odour resistant.

I went for a synthetic top from Sports Direct. The shorts are from Decathlon, they have huge pockets and a belt to keep them from slipping down whilst hiking. I'm not a personal fan of trousers so I don't bring any. If it's cold at night i'll wear my thermal leggings and if it's raining on the trail I don't mind my legs getting wet. Zip off trousers are a good shout if you fancy the choice though.

Valuables

PHONE

Download maps.me Offline maps

You don't need to pay for an offline trail app subscription. I use maps.me for offline trail navigation. It's free and shows me where all the campsites, refugios, grocery shops, ATMs and other points of interests are. I can bookmark points on the map, see the elevation profile and estimated travel time of a route which helps me plan my trip.

I research and download my route's kml / kmz file from Google / All Trails' website. Send that file to my phone via email, instant messenger or cloud storage. Open the file → tap "Share" button → choose "Copy to maps.me" (this saves the route in my maps.me bookmarks for me to use offline whilst out on the trail).

CARD & CASH

Valuables

CARD & CASH

Revolut Travel card

Hiking or not, Revolut is the best travel card! If you don't yet have an account with them, you can download their app on your phone, create an account and have a card posted to you for £5.

I use my Revolut card for all my travel expenses. Their free standard account allows £200 worth of free withdrawals from your destination's local ATM every month. Any withdrawals above this will incur a 2% fee.

Ensure you have enough cash on you before you reach the next town or ATM on your trail. When deciding how much to carry research the cost of food, the number of days and add a little extra for contingency. Goes without saying, also pack a back up card just in case.
